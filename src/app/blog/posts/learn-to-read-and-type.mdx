before you go off to learn your first programming language, learn how to read and type.

sounds absurd, but they are just as important.

## Typing

if you have to look down at your keyboard while typing, you don't know how to type. 

you can't be looking down at the keyboard and see what is being printed on the screen at the same time, it's inefficient, impossible in fact.

while programming, if you have to worry about where the semi colon is on the keyboard, you will never be able to focus on the problem at hand.

if you can't even tell where the comma is on the keyboard, you will never be able to compose extensive documentation.

you get the idea but what's the solution?

**learn touch typing.**

so much so that you should be able to type anything with your eyes closed with > 90% accuracy.

## Reading

you will spend more time reading stuff than you will ever spend creating them.

the faster you get comfortable with reading other people's code, technical documentation, long threads, logs... etc, the faster you will gain knowledge and become better as a developer.

reading is also the fastest way to get information, not every thing will be available to you as a step by step youtube tutorial.

if you have to rely on chatgpt to summarize things for you, how are you going to find the missing semi colon hidden in a 1200+ lines file?

get comfortable with staring at text on your screen, you don't really have a choice.

## Final Verdict

it's very important that you must get both of these basics down very early in your learning journey.

and it won't take years, not even months, to learn these two skills but will save you a lot of your time.

knowing how to read and type is beneficial for everyone, not just people related to cs.
